Who we are

Appleseed is a London-based charity dedicated to promoting reforestation and urban tree planting by awarding decentralised tree credits.

What we do

We facilitate tree planters and tree health checkers by awarding them Appleseed tokens every time they plant or check up on a tree.

Keep track

Have your tree planting organised in one place

Every time you plant a tree, record where, when and which species you planted and send a photo to the Appleseed team. We'll work on verifying your plant, and will then issue you a coin.

Or, you can do a health check on a tree that someone else has already planted. Every six months, planted trees become eligible for a 'health check'. Go to the location of one of these trees, and, if the tree is doing well, send a photo to the team. We'll issue an Appleseed coin to your account!

Carbon credits revolutionised

What's wrong with carbon credits?

  • 'Phantom' trees which may have been cut down, died naturally, or torn down by the elements are counted.
  • Existing forests which would have stayed there in the first place, are rewarded. This is good for protecting woodland, but doesn't incentivise new planting. We need both.
  • Carbon Credits are too simplistic and one-dimensional - trees provide far, far more than just carbon credits! (click here)

    Empowering people and communities to plant trees

    By restoring miniforests and woodland, we all benefit

    Carbon Capture

    Trees store carbon dioxide, a major greenhouse gas, mitigating climate change by absorbing carbon into their woody biomass.

    Cleaner Air

    Breathe easy, trees can absorb and remove pollutants caused by human activity.

    Biodiversity

    Trees provide homes and food for a diverse array of wildlife, supporting ecosystems by creating habitats.

    Temperature moderation

    Providing natural shade means that trees reduce the urban heat island effect, cooling the surrounding environment.

    Flood management

    Tree roots stabilize soil, reducing erosion and preventing sediment runoff into water bodies.

    Community wellbeing

    Access to green spaces and trees has been linked to reduced stress levels and improved mental health outcomes.
